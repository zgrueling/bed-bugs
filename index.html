From rats to roaches, New York City has no shortage of pests. But arguably the most hated of all are bed bugs. The blood-sucking insects, which fully grown resemble apple seeds in shape and color, can live in the seams of mattresses and other upholstered furniture, as well as in the baseboards of walls. Cimex lectularius typically emerge at night to feed on their human hosts, and their bites can leave itchy or painful welts.

While reduced travel during the covid-19 pandemic may have granted New Yorkers some reprieve from the pest, the city still recorded 3,955 reports of bed bug infestations in 2020 alone. The NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development tracks reports of infestations, which are required annually of multi-unit property owners. Analysis of the data reveals a more complicated distribution than one determined solely by population density.

NYC neighborhoods with most reported bed bug infestations in 2020

  1. Flatbush, Brooklyn: 106
  2. Central Harlem North-Polo Grounds, Manhattan: 99
  3. Upper West Side, Manhattan: 98
  4. Washington Heights South, Manhattan: 94
  5. Parkchester, Bronx: 88

Bed bugs are not known to spread disease, but an infestation is a nuisance and can cause extreme psychological distress.* Eradication can also impose a significant financial burden. Individual families commonly spend up to $5000 on ridding their home of the pest, while landlords of large buildings may pay as much as $80,000.

In NYC, property owners are required by law to promptly address bed bug infestations. If their landlord fails to take appropriate actions, tenants should call 311 or file a complaint online.

* Author speaks from personal experience.
